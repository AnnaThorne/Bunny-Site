Bunny types

Blanc de Hotot

Source: link

Hailing from France, the Blanc de Hotot is a medium-sized rabbit breed, with its signature black rings around each eye, their fur is also always white.

Nowadays they're considered an endagered species, but they've been becoming more popular with rabbit owners in recent years.

Lionhead

Source: link

The most recognizable feature of the Lionhead is its soft fluffy mane around the head. They tend to be a bit on the smaller side, but they have a fun personality and are very playful.

Another plus is that they actually have quite a long lifespan - usually around 10 years.

Dutch rabbit

Source: link

Easily recognizable by their fur pattern in the shape of black pants and a white shirt. The Dutch rabbit is one of the most popular rabbit breeds among bunny owners.

They have a friendly and easy-going personality, however, they are especially prone to being distressed from being stuck in a small enclosed space - such as a cage.

Netherland Dwarf

Source: link

The Netherland Dwarf is one of the smallest rabbit breeds. They're round shaped and their furs come in all sorts of colors - they don't have any specific patterns like some of the other breeds.

They're also one of the more intelligent rabbit breeds and are extremely energetic. Lastly, they tend to be prone towards getting stressed and nervous, so they might require some extra attention.

English Lop

Source: link

The English Lop is well known for its long lop ears - actually the longest of any rabbit breed. They have long bodies and come in several colors, the most common one being brown.

They require a lot of room to move around, so that they don't step on their ears. When it comes to personality, they tend to be curious and outgoing.

Checkered Giant

Source: link

As the name suggest, this kind of bunny is one of the largest breeds. Their coats always come in white, with a distinct black pattern around the nose, ears and the back. They're a bit more independent than other breeds, so they don't require as much attention.

They're also gentle and are a good fit for people who don't have as much time to spend with their bunny. However, do keep in mind that, due to their larger size, they might require a bit more room to move around.
