Equipment

Water and food bowls

Bunnies need to have acess to clean drinking water, water bowls are easy for them to drink out of compared to drip feeding water bottles, the kind that you might use for a hamster or other smaller rodents. Ideally you'd get your bunny a heavier bowl made out of ceramics - so that it's not easy to tip over and spill.

Litter tray

A litter tray serves as a dedicated space for your bunny to poop, you should also fill it with some comfortable base for bunnies to sit on such as wood or paper based litter, also make sure that the litter tray is large enough for your bunny to sit on.

Housing

Housing is extremely important to bunnies, as it serves as a place for them to retreat into when they get scared. So it's important that your bunny is provided with a comfortable living enclosure.

An essential part of a bunny's home is comfortable bedding, suitable materials include fresh hay, wood shavings - but no sawdust, straw and shredded paper - ink free.

Ideally you would provide your bunny with an indoor exercise pen with a cozy wooden cube house inside it. You can also combine an indoor pen with a cage as a house for your bunnies to sleep in.

Grooming

Even your bunny needs a grooming kit, which should consist of a comb brush and a nail clipper.

You should brush your bunny's fur once every few days, unless your bunny has longer fur, in which case you'd need to brush it more regularly. Don't apply too much pressure on the brush - just enough to get through their fur and get rid of all the knots and tangles without scratching their skin, also stay away from brushes with metal tips.

As with brushing, you should clip your bunny's nails regularly. Providing your bunnies with hard surfaces to play and run on also helps with keeping their nails trimmed.

Hay rack

A hay rack is essential to keep hay off the floor and it provides your bunnies easy access to it. A good place to put a hay rack would be either next your bunny's litter box or their living enclosure.

Toys

Bunnies are playful creatures, as such they get enjoyment out of simple toys. The best kinds of toys for your bunny are: chew toys, digging boxes, foraging mats, puzzle cups, hideaway tents and climbing towers.
