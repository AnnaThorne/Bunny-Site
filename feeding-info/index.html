Feeding

Hay

Hay makes up the bulk of a bunny's diet - around 80-90%. It's necessary to keep their teeth healthy, and more importantly, it's a source of fiber, which helps bunnies with digestion.

The best kind of hay for adult bunnies is grass hay, oat hay, timothy hay or orchard grass. You can even give your bunny a mixture of different types of hay. Baby bunnies can also consume alfalfa hay, because of it's higher sugar content.

Make sure that the hay is fresh and. doesn't contain mold. The hay should be stored in a dry place, so that it doesn't get wet and moldy. It's also a good idea to leave a source of hay next to your bunny's litterbox, as they like to eat and poop at the same time.

Vegetables

Vegetables are some of the favorite food for bunnies. In general, most greens are safe with the exception of beans, seeds, nuts corn and potatoes - they're difficult for bunnies to digest.

Some of the best vegetables for bunnies include bell peppers, brussel sprouts, cucumbers, herbs, sprouts, wheatgrass, zucchini, celery, dandelion leaves and dark leaf lettuce. These can be safely given to bunnies on a daily basis.

Then there are some vegetables that should be given to bunnies sparingly - once or twice a week: broccoli, carrots, clover, flowers, kale and spinach.

You should introduce your bunny to vegetables slowly, let them try one new veeggie a day and see which ones they like the most. You should also remember that, while bunnies enjoy vegetables and they are healthy for them, they don't replace hay and it should still be their main source of food - vegetables should be used more sparingly, once or twice a day.

Fruit

Similarly to vegetables, you should introduce your bunnies to new fruit slowly and let them decide which types of fruit they like the most. It's also important to note that, you should only give your bunnies fruit one to two times a week, as it is high in sugar.

The best fruits to give to your bunny include: apples, bananas, berries, cherries without seeds, grapes, nectarines, melons, oranges, papayas, peaches, pears, pineapples, plums, watermelon.

In general, bunnies prefer seedless fruit, or fruit with seeds that are small enough to safely consume. Be careful not to give your bunny too much fruit, it's important for them to keep a balanced diet.

Treats

Treats are less about a bunny's diet and more about making it happy and giving it a reward, as such, treats should be used very sparingly.

The healthiest kind of treats for bunnies are pieces of fresh or freeze-dried fruit and mixes of dried flowers. You can also find pre-made bunny treats at pet stores - just check the ingredient list to make sure they're actually safe.

Pellets

Pellets are another kind of bunny food, they act as a supplement to hay, but they should not be used as a replacement. You should only give your bunny pellets with high fiber and low protein, as high protein pellets can cause health issues in bunnies.

Water

As with hay, bunnies should have unrestricted access to clean water, because it's important for them to stay hydrated. A single bunny consumes anywhere from 50 to 150 ml of water per day, which is higher than most animals of its size.
