General tips

Do

Change your bunny's hay and water

You should change your bunny's water supply twice a day and provide it with fresh hay on a daily basis. Hay is extremely important for bunnies as a source of fiber - helps with digestion and it helps to keep their teeth in a healthy shape. The best kind of hay is grass or oat hay.

Pay attention to your bunny's diet

As mentioned before, hay is actually the most important part of a healthy bunny's diet, however, it's not the only part of it. You should also feed your bunny a healthy mix of vegetables and fruit such as broccoli, melon, bananas, dark leaf lettuce, apples and of course carrots. Hay pellets can also be given to bunnies in smaller quantities. Lastly, don't forget to give your bunny treats from time to time - freeze dried fruit and dried flowers.

Make sure that your bunny has access to clean water

This might sound obvious, but it's actually really important for a bunny to have access to clean water, they get more easily dehydrated compared to other species of their size - an average bunny consumes more water than a dog. So it's definitely not something to skip out on.

Give your bunny enough room

Bunnies are active animals that need a lot of room for them to healthily exercise and play in. The best possible evnironment for them would be a sectioned off area of your house, specifically for your bunnies - yes even an entire room. Providing them access to your garden is also a good idea, however, be wary of wild predators as bunnies can't defend themselves.

Don't

Leave your bunny alone

Bunnies are social creatures that rely on interaction to keep them from being lonely and bored. A single bunny can be left alone for up to 12 hours, afterwards it can become distressed and suffer. However, if you have two or more bunnies, they'll be able to keep each other company for up to a weekend.

Keep your bunny in a cage

While it's ok to put your bunny in a carrying cage for a short vet visit, cages aren't a suitable evnironment for them to be in for extended periods of time. Bunnies need enough space to comfortably run around and exercise. Keeping them in a cage long-term has detrimental effects to their physical but also mental health, being stuck in a small enclosed space just isn't fun to them.

Feed your bunny human food

While bunnies can consume most fruit and vegetables, they aren't allowed to eat regular human food and treats. That means no chocolate, no cookies or crackers, no pasta, no sugar, no yogurt, no beans and no cereal.

Put your bunny in a loud environment

Bunnies are naturally prey animals, that means they are prone to getting scared and wanting to hide. Which also means they are easily scared by loud noises, however, bunnies can get adjusted to louder evnironments if they feel like there is no direct threat to them.
